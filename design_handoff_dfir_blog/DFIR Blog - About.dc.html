WHY FORENSICS

It started with a question I couldn’t let go of: when you delete a file, where does it go? The answer — mostly nowhere — pulled me into filesystems, then memory, then networks. Forensics turned out to be the discipline where curiosity is the job description: every image is a crime scene where the evidence can’t lie, only hide.

I learn in public on purpose. Every claim on this site is dated and reproducible — the field notes include the dead ends, and the toolbox scores are backed by named challenges, not vibes. If I write that I know a tool 4/10, that’s a promise about the other 6 too.

What I’d bring on day one: solid fundamentals in crypto and network protocols, hands-on habits with the standard toolchain, documentation discipline — and the kind of stubbornness that reads the RFC when the blog post doesn’t add up.
